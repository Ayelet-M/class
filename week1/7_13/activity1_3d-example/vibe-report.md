## 🎯 The Vibe (Planning)
The goal was to create a 3D "Link Tree" that feels premium, atmospheric, and deeply integrated with my hardware-centric PhD research. I wanted to move away from flat 2D grids and into a spatial journey. The inspiration was high-end automotive configurators and Apple-style product landing pages—using dark glassmorphism and light to guide the eye.

## 👣 Process & Explorations
Transparency is key. 
- **Iteration 1**: Started with abstract spheres, but it felt too generic. I realized I needed personal objects (the Van, the Piggy Bank) to ground the "vibe."
- **Pathways**: Explored using `drei/ScrollControls`, but hit a wall with DOM syncing. Switched to `r3f-scroll-rig` to get the snappy, hardware-accelerated feel.
- **Dropped Ideas**: Initially wanted a full 3D room, but it distracted from the links. I dropped the room in favor of a "glow" that changes per section.

## 💭 Reflection

1. **How did vibe coding change your approach?**  
   It allowed me to focus on the transitions and the feeling of depth without getting bogged down in the math of individual lerps. I could describe the "snappy" feeling I wanted, and the AI handled the damping and threshold adjustments.
2. **Structural Elements Borrowed:**  
   The "scroll-linked animation" paradigm common in high-end product landing pages. Attribution for 3D models is included in the footer.
3. **Surprising Lessons:**  
   The AI was surprisingly good at translating aesthetic descriptions like "frosted glass" into specific CSS backdrop-filter combinations.
4. **Final Vision & Authenticity:**  
   The final site matches my original vision of a "digital twin." It feels authenticated by the custom models and the lighting derived from my actual asset palette.
