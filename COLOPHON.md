# AI Colophon

This repository is itself an example of the course's core practice: building with AI while documenting what the AI did.

## Course Materials

Most public course materials in this repository were drafted, edited, reorganized, or checked with **OpenAI Codex** under instructor direction and review. That includes:

- assignment instructions in `week1/`, `week2/`, and `week3/`;
- group-project briefs and report templates in `projects/`;
- repository setup files such as `AGENTS.md`, `CLAUDE.md`, `GEMINI.md`, Cursor/Windsurf/Replit rule mirrors, and related agent instructions;
- site pages such as `index.html`, `projects/index.html`, and `instructions.html`;
- maintenance and student-support docs such as `README.md`, `STARTHERE.md`, `DEPLOY.md`, `SUBMISSION.md`, and `MAINTAINING.md`;
- deployment and utility scripts such as `.github/workflows/deploy.yml` and files under `scripts/`.

The instructors selected the course structure, learning goals, assignments, policies, readings, grading expectations, and final wording. Codex helped turn those decisions into files, revise inconsistencies, and keep the repository wired together.

## Human Review

AI-generated or AI-assisted text and code in the course materials should be treated as instructor-reviewed, not autonomous output. The instructors remain responsible for the published course materials, including any errors that made it through review.

## Student Work

Student submissions have their own provenance requirements. Each activity or project must include:

- `log_deliverable/history.md`, the local Vibe-Trace log;
- any external AI chat/tool links or transcripts used;
- the model/tool names used where known, for example `GitHub Copilot`, `OpenAI Codex`, `ChatGPT GPT-5`, `Claude`, `Gemini`, `v0`, `Replit`, or image/audio/video generators.

Do not cite this course colophon as the provenance for your own assignment. It only describes the construction of the course repository.
